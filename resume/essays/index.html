THEA 316   |   Wesleyan University

Performance Studies

Prof. Cláudia Tatinge Nascimento

In-depth research and discussion of performance in different cultures using theoretical lenses Erika Fischer-Lichte introduces in The Transformative Power of Performance: A New Aesthetics. I kickstarted my research with materials from another course, Politics in Japan, and wrote about performative elements in Japan's judicial system.

18 May 2017 — The People in the Place


GOVT 296   |   Wesleyan University

Politics in Japan

Prof. Mary Alice Haddad

Overview of the last one-hundred years of Japanese political and cultural history. We focused on the Liberal Democratic Party's long-lasting reign, and I analyzed the future of the LDP for the course midterm. The course also necessitated a twenty-page research paper, and I wrote about Japan's economic role in the burgeoning video game industry. I used several theoretical frames and market data to analyze mostly Nintendo software and hardware.

18 May 2017 — An Industry in Flux... 27 Feb 2017 — Essay Response


ENGL 281   |   Wesleyan University

Award-Winning Playwrights

Prof. Rashida Shaw McMahon

Reading and analysis of critically acclaimed contemporary plays, including works from Lynn Nottage, Tony Kushner, Suzan-Lori Parks, and Tracy Letts. My essays discuss and compare themes of or between various playwrights, and my final research paper is a holistic overview of the Pulitzer Prize for Drama.

16 Dec 2016 — Award-Winning Playwrights 20 Nov 2016 — Love Interrupted 16 Oct 2016 — At War in the Deepest Trench 5 Sept 2016 — Gently


THEA 203   |   Wesleyan University

Special Topics in Theater History

Prof. Katherine Brewer Ball

Global study of various types of theater and performance, pairing theoretical essays on different cultures and theatrical disciplines with plays. The course's papers consist of two performance reviews, one in-class presentation on an assigned reading, and a companion paper to an in-class performance.

9 Dec 2016 — Wesleyan Poem About Dog Education 30 Nov 2016 — The Loudest Voice in the Room 28 Sept 2016 — Nothing Disappears 26 Spet 2016 — Talk


FILM 307   |   Wesleyan University

The Language of Hollywood

Prof. Scott Higgins

Historic rundown of Hollywood's aesthetic development, progressing from recorded sound, to color, to widescreen, to current "digital revolutions" such as 3D. Each essay is a response to a film viewing and responds to the technological development pioneered or emphasized in the respective film.

19 Dec 2015 — What Are We Doing? 18 Nov 2015 — The Empty Space 19 Oct 2015 — Talking and Tapping and Singing and Dancing


ARHA 182   |   Wesleyan University

Arts of Imperial China

Prof. Yun-chiahn Chen Sena

Focus on several art forms in Imperial China, including courtly paintings, calligraphy, and blue-and-white porcelains. These papers are my first forays ito visual art critique and analysis as well as new vocabulary with which to comment on form and function of physical objects. I concurrently took a course in Italian Art & Architecture, which gave me further insight into this type of writing.

17 Dec 2015 — A Study of an "Owl-Shaped Wine Vessel (Zun)" 1 Dec 2015 — The Allure of Chinese Blue-and-White Porcelains 29 Oct 2015 — Nymph of the Luo River  in Three Styles 1 Oct 2015 — Caparisoned Horse, A Study


THEA 150   |   Wesleyan University

Plays & Performances

Prof. Cláudia Tatinge Nascimento

Theatrical study with a focus on the historical avant-garde and the evolution of theatrical genres, styles, and canons. The course heavily emphasized essay style and quality, and these papers reflect a significant improvement in my writing as I became accustomed to college writing expectations.

15 Dec 2015 — In Motion 6 Dec 2015 — We're Somewhere 15 Nov 2015 — Why Do We Talk? 7 Oct 2015 — Children 23 Sept 2015 — Something Happened and I'm in Love
