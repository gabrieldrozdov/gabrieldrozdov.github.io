1.

Background

The 24 Hour Plays is a producing company that specializes in time-limited theater. In just twenty-four hours, writers, directors, and actors write, rehearse, and perform original musical or plays on Broadway, Off-Broadway, or elsewhere. The company organizes celebrities and various other theater professionals to feature in several events each year, while also sponsoring licensed productions nationally and internationally.

What started as a passion project for the company’s founders soon became an annual and seasonal event that frequently featured A-class celebrities seeking a fun outlet to experiment with new work. I joined The 24 Hour Plays when the company was preparing for its The 24 Hour Musicals: Los Angeles event as well as its intensive developing artist program, The 24 Hour Plays: Nationals. The company initially hired me to assist with administrative work during this preparation period, but my responsibilities quickly spilled into greater marketing and branding projects.

2.

The Problem

Despite the very clear and unified mission statement of The 24 Hour Plays, the company’s branding was disorganized and inconsistent when I joined. The company relied on the celebrity status of each event’s participants by producing ads featuring said celebrities’ headshots and names. While not ineffective, this tactic had little means to unite different marketing graphics into the same brand except for superimposing a logo on each image. On top of all of this, the company’s website was outdated and difficult to maintain because of a poorly implemented back-end. All considered, marketing developments were at a standstill because they proved too time-consuming and expensive to pursue on top of producing new shows.

After I joined the company as an administrative intern, I saw the company’s need for a designer but inability to hire one because of financial boundaries. I approached my boss about the issue and eventually presented a mockup of a website that featured a new brand aesthetic—this initial draft ultimately evolved into The 24 Hour Plays’ current website and branding. In this process, I had to address numerous issues including resolving the website’s back-end, rebuilding every webpage from scratch, composing a manual for updating the site, and building a style guide for the development of future graphics.
